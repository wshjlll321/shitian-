# Visual Rules

## Rule Intent

These rules control the visual identity of the new Shitian UAV website. The site must feel like a domestic premium low-altitude economy brand, not a generic technology website.

## Mandatory Rules

- The visual system must express premium Chinese industrial technology.
- The first viewport must establish brand authority and product presence.
- Use massive product visuals, real operation scenes, aerial scale, industrial detail, terrain, sea, forest, field, and rescue imagery.
- Use Eastern restraint and whitespace: quiet, deliberate, precise, and confident.
- Visual hierarchy must be cinematic: wide establishing visuals, product close-ups, data moments, scenario proof, and calm conversion.
- Product imagery must feel physical, engineered, and real.
- Scene imagery must show real operational context, not abstract atmosphere.
- The visual system must support government, enterprise, agricultural, forestry, emergency, maritime, and overseas audiences.
- Visual contrast must be strong but not aggressive.
- Color must feel premium, industrial, and restrained.

## Forbidden Rules

- Do not inherit old website colors, layout, banner style, product list style, sidebar style, or CMS visual patterns.
- Do not use cheap blue technology styling.
- Do not use blue-purple neon gradients as a dominant identity.
- Do not use particle oceans, hexagons, circuit boards, glowing grids, wireframe globes, fake radar, or fake HUD decoration.
- Do not use full-screen decorative AI backgrounds.
- Do not use meaningless icons as primary communication.
- Do not use stock-like dark blurred technology images when real product or case imagery is available.
- Do not use generic SaaS dashboard visuals.
- Do not use ecommerce product-detail aesthetics.
- Do not use childish illustrations, cartoon drones, or decorative mascot-style assets.
- Do not make every section visually equal.
- Do not make every section the same height.

## Required Visual Language

Preferred visual references:

- Premium EV launch pages
- Huawei high-end product pages
- DJI Enterprise scenario pages
- Apple product storytelling
- Industrial product photography
- Documentary field operation footage

Allowed visual directions:

- Graphite black
- Warm white
- Metal gray
- Low-saturation military green
- Safety orange as controlled accent
- Deep carbon
- Fine technical linework only when tied to real specifications
- Large product media
- Sparse data overlays

## Failure Conditions

Visual direction fails review if:

- It could be mistaken for a template foreign trade website.
- It feels like a SaaS landing page.
- It relies on blue neon to look technological.
- It uses decorative tech symbols with no product meaning.
- It does not show real aircraft or real tasks early enough.
- It lacks premium whitespace.
- It looks AI-generated, generic, or interchangeable.

## Generation Constraints

- Every visual module must state its content purpose before implementation.
- Hero must use real product or real scenario media, not abstract gradients.
- Product and scenario visuals must be more important than icon decoration.
- Media areas must have stable aspect ratios and responsive cropping rules.
- Text must never cover important product structures or task evidence.
