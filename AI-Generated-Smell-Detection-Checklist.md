# AI Generated Smell Detection Checklist

Use this checklist before approving any page, component, visual direction, or copy draft.

If any high-risk item is present, revise before implementation or delivery.

## Brand Specificity

- Does the page still make sense if the company name is removed?
- Could this page belong to any AI, robotics, SaaS, cloud, or smart manufacturing company?
- Are T280, S270, H15, H60, or HIZONE visible in meaningful ways?
- Are real Shitian UAV cases, locations, tasks, or specifications present?
- Does the page communicate low-altitude economy through real scenarios rather than slogans?

## Copy Smell

- Does the copy use vague phrases like "empower the future", "redefine intelligence", "infinite possibilities", or "technology leads innovation"?
- Are there claims without product, case, or data support?
- Are specifications dumped without explaining operational meaning?
- Does every section use the same "title + subtitle + CTA" pattern?
- Could the headings be generated for almost any technology company?

## Visual Smell

- Are there blue-purple gradients, neon glows, particle backgrounds, hexagons, wireframe globes, fake HUDs, or glowing borders?
- Is the design relying on abstract technology atmosphere instead of real products and scenes?
- Are icons carrying content that should be carried by cases, media, or data?
- Does the page look like a SaaS dashboard or foreign trade template?
- Are all sections equal height or visually equal weight?

## Layout Smell

- Is the page mostly repeated three-column cards?
- Are cards nested inside cards?
- Could the sections be rearranged without changing the story?
- Is there no cinematic pause or large product moment?
- Does the page lack a clear path from brand authority to product proof to inquiry?

## Motion Smell

- Are elements flying, bouncing, spinning, pulsing, or glowing without purpose?
- Does animation slow down reading?
- Does motion exist because the page otherwise feels empty?
- Does scrolling feel hijacked?
- Is reduced motion ignored?

## Three.js Smell

- Does the 3D scene exist mainly to look futuristic?
- Does removing the Three.js scene leave product understanding unchanged?
- Is it using particles, neon grids, wireframe earth, or abstract geometry?
- Does it compete with real product media?
- Is there no mobile or static fallback?

## Conversion Smell

- Is the CTA generic, such as "learn more" or "contact us", without context?
- Does the site use "buy now", "hot product", "low price", or similar wording?
- Does the form fail to ask about scenario, area, payload, product, or cooperation type?
- Can users reach inquiry from product, scenario, and case pages?
- Is overseas cooperation unsupported or unclear?

## Final Decision

Approve only if:

- The page feels specific to Shitian UAV.
- Real products and real operational proof dominate.
- The tone is premium, Chinese, industrial, and restrained.
- There is no cheap tech styling.
- Three.js, motion, and UI all serve product storytelling.
- Inquiry paths are clear and professional.
